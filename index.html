Methodology: Integrated SDG & Finance Model

This predictive dashboard bridges fully-burdened structural finance with global best-practices in UN Sustainable Development Goal (SDG) tracking. The model is scientifically underpinned by two primary theoretical frameworks:

1. Activity-Based Costing (ABC) in Higher Education

Traditional university accounting often obscures the true cost of operating a department by lumping indirect costs into general institutional overhead. This tool employs principles of Activity-Based Costing by assigning fully loaded operating costs—including direct personnel encumbrances, program-specific facilities, and a calculated 'Central Tax'—directly against the revenue generated by the unit. This mathematically exposes cross-subsidization and reveals the true localized margin.

2. Triple Bottom Line (TBL) Accounting

A university's performance cannot be evaluated purely on a corporate P&L construct. We adapt the Triple Bottom Line framework (Profit, People, Planet) into a dual-axis scoring indicator combining the Financial Margin (Profit) with the UN SDG Impact Rating (People & Planet). The dynamic institutional slider allows universities to define their unique TBL weighting.

SDG Alignment Metrics for Higher Ed

The Societal Return on Investment (SROI) is quantified via specific SDG proxies mapping to university operations:

  • SDG 4 (Quality & Inclusive Education): Translates to access. Evaluates the percentage of the student body that is first-generation, Pell-eligible, or historically underrepresented (scored 0-100).
  • SDG 8 (Decent Work & Econ Growth): Translates to direct local engagement. Evaluates community service, pro-bono consulting, and local workforce upskilling hours generated by the department.
  • SDG 9 (Innovation): Evaluates knowledge transfer. Tracks the quantity of Open Access publications, patents, or direct policy briefs distributed annually.
  • SDG 13 (Climate Action): Penalizes departments operating heavily carbon-intensive legacy footprints without proactive curriculum or operational mitigation.

Algorithmic Logic & Estimation Protocol

To reduce initial data-entry friction, omitted fields leverage standardized predictive algorithms based on higher ed heuristics. The system generates the dynamic 5-year trendline from your Year 1 snapshot data using the following specific rules:

  • Compounding Attrition: The 5-year projection is not a flat line. A dynamic drop-out rate (calculated as 100 - Retention%) is explicitly subtracted year-over-year from your enrolled base. Alongside a standard graduation cohort exit formula (25% UG and 50% Grad transition out annually), new intake is modeled with a default 2% enrollment growth basis.
  • Inflationary Multipliers: The automated projection applies a 3% compounding inflationary rate to both tuition yield and salary expenditures to simulate realistic fiscal drag, merit increases, and market conditions over the 60-month horizon.
  • Heuristics for Missing Data: If personnel inputs are left blank, the system assumes an optimal 1:20 Full-Time Faculty-to-Student ratio (with equivalent staff ratios) and standard campus climate compliance baselines.
